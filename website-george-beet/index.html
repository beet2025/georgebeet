About George

George is an actor and teacher based in London. He has a diverse career with a balance of performing and teaching.

George trained at Bird College where he graduated in 2015. His performance credits include Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat (UK & Ireland Tour) where he was 1st cover to the title role. George was fortunate enough to play Joseph a number of times across several cities. Previous to this, George was cast in 42nd Street in London’s West End at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane where he spent a year in the show and also appeared in the film that was brought out in Cinemas in November 2019.

Additionally, he performed with the cast at the 2018 Olivier Awards at the Royal Albert Hall. Other credits include ‘Pepper’ in Mamma Mia for Royal Caribbean International; Rimbaud in La Boheme at the Royal Opera House, which was broadcast live in cinema’s worldwide; The Brit Awards, dancing for Kanye West; Dancer/ Understudy Dick Whittington for QDos in High Wycombe, Prince Charming in Cinderella (Poole) and Dancer in Snow White (Norwich Theatre Royal).

Teaching Career

George has been teaching since the age of 16. He was a member of Tap Attack in 2010/2011 and received two silver medals at the World Tap Championships in Germany. After completing the ISTD tap syllabus at 15 years old he started teaching Tap at NAPA (Northern Academy of Performing Arts) in Hull where he trained for 9 years. He found a passion for teaching at this early age and knew he wanted to build it into his career. In 2017, George joined the Tap faculty at the SylviaYoung Theatre School in London where he teaches both at the full time school and the part time school.He has also taught Jazz and choreographed on the Musical Theatre Summer courses.

=

George also taught for a year at the GSA Saturday School in Guildford. He has since taught at a large number of dance schools across London. In January 2020 George joined the faculty at Bird College as a guest tutor, specialising in Tap, Jazz and Musical Theatre and choreographed for the 3rd year’s agent showcase. George is extremely passionate about giving his students the highest standard of training and also passing on his expertise from the knowledge that he’s gained by working in the industry. George recently started his own online teaching platform, Online Performing Academy. For more information on this please head to the Online Performing Academy section.

Online Performing Academy

Recently, George has created his own online teaching platform,Online Performing Academy (OPA), where he teaches a range of genres through Zoom. OPA offers classes in all styles as well as West End workshops and 1-2-1 training. George and his team teach students all over the UK and have even managed to reach out to students in Norway, Germany, South Africa, America and Canada.
